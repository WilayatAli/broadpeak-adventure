K2 Base Camp Trek Overview

The trek to K2 base camp typically takes around 14-20 days and covers a distance of approximately 100 kilometres. The trek starts from the village of Askole in the Skardu district of Pakistan and follows the Braldu River up to the Baltoro glacier, which leads to the base camp.

The trek involves several high-altitude passes and steep climbs, making it a challenging trek that requires physical fitness and previous trekking experience. The highest point on the trek is the Concordia, a plateau that lies at an altitude of 4,700 meters and offers breathtaking views of K2 and several other peaks.


The route to the base camp passes through several small villages and settlements, providing an opportunity to interact with the local people and experience their unique culture and way of life. The Balti people who live in the area are known for their hospitality and warmth, and the trek provides an opportunity to learn about their traditions and customs.

Preparation

Before embarking on the base camp trek, it is essential to be physically fit and have previous trekking experience. The trek involves several high-altitude passes and steep climbs, and it is important to have a good level of fitness to avoid altitude sickness and other health issues.

It is also important to have proper gear and equipment, including warm clothing, a sturdy backpack, good quality trekking shoes, and a sleeping bag suitable for cold temperatures at high altitudes. It is recommended to carry a first-aid kit and other essential items such as sunscreen, insect repellent, and a water purification system.

Permits

To undertake the K2 base camp trek, it is necessary to obtain a trekking permit from the Pakistani government. The permit can be obtained through a registered travel agency in Pakistan, and it is important to carry a copy of the permit at all times during the trek.
